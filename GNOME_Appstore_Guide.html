How to Install the GNOME Appstore on Your Chromebook

Description:

This guide will help you install an app called, "GNOME Software". In this guide "GNOME Software" will be refered to as, "GNOME’s appstore" for simplicity. The GNOME’s appstore is a really easy to use appstore by GNOME. GNOME’s appstore provides a simple UI for downloading and installing apps that use GNOME’s theme system. If you have followed the Dark Theme Guide apps installed from here should have a dark or custom theme by default.

Requirements:

Recommended Requirements:

Press Ctrl+Search+Esc to find this:

  • 4GB+ RAM
  • Dual-core Intel or AMD CPU at a minimum of 2.50 GHz

Mandatory Requirements:

  • Linux VM set up, and updated
  • 8GB+ of free storage in Linux VM
  • 15 minutes of time

Directions:

Step 1: Open the terminal and type in, "sudo apt-get upgrade -y" and press enter.

Tip: To paste commands into the terminal, just right-click the command line.

Step 2: Type in, "sudo apt install gnome-software -y" and press enter.

Step 3: Once it is done, open the laucher and click on an app called, "Software"

Step 4: Download some apps! Preferable download apps made by The GNOME Project, as they will have support for custom themes and are secure.
